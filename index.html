Zuni Jewelry

Zuni jewelry is known for its intricate inlay and delicate stonework, featuring petit point, needlepoint and inlaid designs set in both silver and gold. The Zuni learned silversmithing from the Navajo in in the late 1800's, developing their own style during the early 20th century.

Inlay Work

Zuni inlay jewelry consists of intricate mosaic-like stonework, precisely cut and set into into silver or gold.

Stones used in Zuni inlay

Zuni inlay features stones such as turquoise, jet and coral. Older pieces may also feature tortoise shell, which was banned in 1977.
